More About Me

Education

Even though I switched out of the pre-medicine track, I still have the drive to help others. I have started pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science where I will learn more about data structures, algorithms, and all the tools necessary to succeed as a software engineer. However, I will continue to remember the skills I learned from my pursuits in Neuroscience and Public Health.

Public Health has taught me to look into both upstream and downstream issues that allow me to assess the root of the problem at hand. This skill will be translated in the software industry; for example, I will be mindful of how a single line of code could affect the rest of the system. Therefore, I aim to write intentional and good code. Additionally, Public Health has taught me about cultural competence, so I could deliver solutions to multiple demographics while keeping the intended audience in mind. This will apply in the software industry by ensuring my colleagues will be able to understand my code without having to exert extreme effort.
